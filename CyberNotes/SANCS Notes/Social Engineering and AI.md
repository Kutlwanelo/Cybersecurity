## 1️⃣ Core Idea

Most cyber attacks don’t start with hacking systems.

They start with **hacking people**.

This is called **social engineering** — manipulating people into revealing information or performing actions that help attackers.

The lecturer called it:

> **“The art of human hacking.”**

---

# 2️⃣ Why Cybersecurity Is Still a Problem

The lecturer mentioned several reasons:

**1. Software complexity**

- Millions of lines of code
    
- Many programming languages
    
- Third-party libraries
    

Even **small mistakes** can create vulnerabilities.

**2. Attackers only need one mistake**  
Defenders must secure everything.

Attackers only need **one weakness**.

**3. Security often seen as a cost**  
Historically organizations prioritized:

- features
    
- revenue
    

instead of security.

**4. Human mistakes**  
People:

- reuse passwords
    
- click links
    
- misconfigure systems
    

Humans are not perfect.

---

# 3️⃣ Social Engineering (The Human Attack)

Social engineering works by exploiting **trust and psychology**.

Attackers try to:

- influence
    
- manipulate
    
- deceive
    
- build trust
    

so victims perform actions themselves.

Examples:

- revealing passwords
    
- clicking malicious links
    
- downloading malware
    

---

# 4️⃣ Common Social Engineering Attacks

### Phishing

Fake emails pretending to be trusted organizations.

Goal:

- steal credentials
    
- trick victims into clicking malicious links.
    

---

### Spear Phishing

More targeted phishing attacks aimed at:

- specific people
    
- specific companies.
    

---

### Whaling

Targets **high-level executives** (CEO, CFO).

These victims often have access to sensitive systems or money.

---

### Smishing

Phishing using **SMS messages**.

---

### Vishing

Phishing using **voice calls**.

AI voice cloning is making this more dangerous.

---

### QR Phishing

Malicious links hidden inside QR codes.

These bypass some email security filters.

---

# 5️⃣ How Social Engineering Attacks Work

The lecturer explained a **3-step process**:

### Step 1 — Reconnaissance

Attackers research the victim.

Sources:

- LinkedIn
    
- social media
    
- company websites
    
- leaked data
    

---

### Step 2 — The Hook

Attackers craft a message designed to grab attention.

This message exploits human psychology.

---

### Step 3 — The Catch

The victim:

- clicks the link
    
- opens attachment
    
- shares credentials.
    

---

# 6️⃣ Psychological Tricks Attackers Use

Attackers exploit **principles of influence**:

### Authority

Pretending to be:

- IT department
    
- manager
    
- bank
    

---

### Trust

Using known names or companies.

Example:  
Email appears to come from a colleague.

---

### Fear

Example message:

> “Your account will be locked if you don’t act immediately.”

---

### Urgency

Victim feels pressured to act quickly.

---

### Social Proof

Message claims:

> “Everyone in the department has completed this form.”

Victim follows the crowd.

---

# 7️⃣ Cognitive Bias (Mental Shortcuts)

Humans use **mental shortcuts** to process information quickly.

Attackers exploit these biases.

Examples mentioned:

### Habit bias

If something looks routine, we don’t question it.

Example:  
Monthly email from HR.

---

### Halo effect

We trust familiar brands or organizations.

Example:  
Email from Microsoft or company domain.

---

### Loss aversion

People react strongly to possible financial loss.

Example:  
“Your bank account is compromised.”

---

### Curiosity effect

People want to know what’s inside a message.

Example:  
“You have won a prize.”

---

# 8️⃣ How AI Is Making Social Engineering Worse

Artificial intelligence is changing cyber attacks dramatically.

---

## AI Helps Attackers

### 1. Reconnaissance

AI can gather information about targets quickly.

Example:  
Ask a chatbot about a person or company.

---

### 2. Writing Phishing Emails

AI generates perfect grammar and realistic messages.

Old detection methods (bad spelling) no longer work.

---

### 3. Personalization

Attackers can create **highly targeted emails** using data from:

- LinkedIn
    
- social media
    
- company websites.
    

---

### 4. Malware Generation

AI can help attackers create malicious code.

---

### 5. Faster Attacks

The lecturer mentioned the **5-5 rule**:

> 5 prompts in 5 minutes can generate a phishing campaign.

---

# 9️⃣ Deepfakes (Very Dangerous)

Deepfakes are **AI-generated media**:

- fake audio
    
- fake video
    
- fake images.
    

Example mentioned:

A **video conference attack** where everyone in the meeting except the victim was deepfake.

Result:

**$25 million stolen.**

---

# 10️⃣ Why Tools Alone Are Not Enough

Traditional defenses struggle because:

- AI can change attacks quickly
    
- phishing messages look perfect
    
- deepfakes look real.
    

The lecturer emphasized:

> **Soft skills are now critical.**

---

# 11️⃣ Important Defensive Mindset

The most important defense strategy:

### Verify First

If you receive a request:

Ask yourself:

- Do I know this person?
    
- Am I expecting this message?
    
- Am I involved in this project?
    

If unsure:

**verify through another channel**

Example:

- call the person
    
- message them on Teams.
    

---

# 12️⃣ Key Soft Skills for Cybersecurity

The lecturer emphasized:

### Critical Thinking

Pause before reacting.

---

### Radical Skepticism

Do not trust messages automatically.

---

### Verification

Confirm requests through another method.

---

### Awareness of Digital Footprint

Information you share online can be used against you.

---

# 13️⃣ Important Mindset Shift

The lecturer made a key point:

Humans are **not the weakest link**.

Humans are part of the system.

Security systems fail when they assume humans are **perfect**.

---

# 14️⃣ How This Connects to SOC Work

SOC analysts deal with these attacks daily.

Typical SOC tasks include:

### Detecting Phishing Campaigns

Monitoring suspicious emails.

---

### Investigating Alerts

Checking if a user clicked a malicious link.

---

### Incident Response

If a user falls for phishing:

- reset credentials
    
- isolate device
    
- investigate attacker activity.
    

---

### Security Awareness

SOC teams often help train employees.

---

# 3 Most Important Takeaways

1️⃣ **Social engineering targets humans, not systems.**

2️⃣ **AI is making social engineering attacks more powerful.**

3️⃣ **Critical thinking and verification are now essential security skills.**
